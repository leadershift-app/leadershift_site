Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know, from privacy to pricing.

Can my department see my conversations?
No. This is the foundation of everything we built. Your conversations are never logged and you are never identified. We have no backdoors for administrators or agencies. There is no record for your department to access and nothing that can be subpoenaed through departmental channels. You can delete your data at any time and it is wiped completely. This is not a policy. It is how the system is architected.
What is LeaderShift?
LeaderShift is an AI-powered peer support and professional development tool built specifically for public safety professionals. It helps you navigate career progression, leadership challenges, and the daily realities of the job with guidance that understands rank structures, department culture, and the unique pressures you face.
Is this therapy?
No. LeaderShift is a peer support and professional development tool, not licensed therapy. It focuses on career development, leadership skills, and navigating workplace challenges. If you are dealing with trauma, PTSD, or clinical issues, we encourage you to work with a licensed professional. Many people find professional development and clinical care complement each other well.
How is this different from ChatGPT?
We built LeaderShift because generic AI fails public safety professionals. Our testing showed tools like ChatGPT routinely give advice that conflicts with standard policing practices, ignores chain of command, or misses critical safety considerations. LeaderShift understands what a Sergeant is, how a Captain communicates differently with a Chief than with a Rookie, and the specific realities of your work environment. We speak to officers, not about them.
How is this different from ChatGPT?
We built LeaderShift because generic AI fails public safety professionals. Our testing showed tools like ChatGPT routinely give advice that conflicts with standard agency practices, misses organizational hierarchy, or overlooks compliance considerations. LeaderShift understands organizational dynamics, how a division director's challenges differ from those of a new analyst, and the specific realities of working in a public safety environment. We speak to professionals, not about them.
Will using this affect my fitness-for-duty status?
No. LeaderShift is a professional development tool. Using it is no different from reading a leadership book or attending a conference. It does not create clinical records, and since your department has no access to your conversations, your use cannot be factored into fitness-for-duty evaluations or affect your career standing.
Will my employer or agency know I'm using this?
No. LeaderShift is a personal professional development tool. Using it is no different from reading a leadership book or attending a conference on your own time. Your agency has no access to your conversations, and your use leaves no trace in any workplace system.
I'm a patrol officer, not a supervisor. Is this for me?
Yes. Leadership development does not start at promotion. Some of the most consequential career decisions happen at the patrol level: whether to go for Sergeant, how to handle a difficult FTO relationship, how to build the skills that promotion boards actually look for, and how to stay motivated when the job gets heavy. LeaderShift meets you where you are.
I'm not in a management role. Is this for me?
Yes. Professional development does not start at a supervisory title. Some of the most consequential career decisions happen when you are in individual contributor roles: how to build influence without positional authority, how to navigate organizational politics, how to advocate for your unit during reorganizations, and how to position yourself for advancement. LeaderShift meets you where you are.
How does LeaderShift benefit the communities officers serve?
When public safety professionals invest in their own leadership development, the effects ripple outward. Better communication skills, more thoughtful decision-making, stronger self-awareness, and greater resilience under pressure all translate directly into how officers interact with the people they serve. Supporting the people who carry the badge is one of the most direct ways to strengthen public safety for everyone.

Better communication, more thoughtful decision-making, and greater resilience translate directly into how agencies operate. Is my data used to train AI models?
No. We do not train our AI on your personal conversations. Your data stays your data. Personalization happens within your individual experience, not by feeding your information into a broader training dataset.

The Challenge

Modern policing demands leaders it does not fully equip.

The profession asks officers to be crisis negotiators, community liaisons, legal scholars, team managers, media-facing representatives, and tactical operators, often within the same shift. Officers do receive training as they advance. But there is a gap between what formal programs deliver and what officers actually need to navigate the complex organizational realities of their careers.

That gap widens at every step up the chain of command. The higher you go, the fewer people are available to talk to honestly about what the role actually demands.

The people who need the most strategic support have the fewest confidential places to pursue it. That is the paradox of leadership in public safety.

Modern public safety agencies demand professionals they do not fully equip.

The work asks staff to be data analysts, compliance experts, technology managers, budget stewards, and cross-functional liaisons, often across multiple divisions. Non-sworn professionals do receive training. But there is a gap between what formal programs deliver and what professionals actually need to navigate complex organizational careers in public safety environments.

That gap widens at every step up the organizational hierarchy. The higher you go, the fewer people are available to talk to honestly about what the role actually demands.

The people who need the most strategic support have the fewest confidential places to pursue it. That is the paradox of leadership in public safety.

The Gap

Generic AI fails public safety professionals.

Officers need timely guidance on complex conversations, space to decompress after a hard shift, and the ability to reorient when switching between professional demands and the rest of their lives. Generic tools were not designed for any of this.

Agency professionals need timely guidance on complex conversations, space to decompress after a demanding week, and the ability to reorient when switching between professional demands and the rest of their lives. Generic tools were not designed for any of this.
